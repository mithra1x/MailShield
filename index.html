The extension runs entirely in your browser. Open any email in Gmail, click Scan current email, and get a 0–100 risk score with a clear Low / Medium / High level and a list of suspicious URLs with reasons.

Risk is built from urgency phrases, credential/OTP-style language, attachment bait, and per-link checks: dangerous schemes, non-HTTPS, IP-based URLs, shorteners, domain mismatch (visible text vs. real destination), and lookalike domains (e.g. paypa1.com).

Key features

Smart scanning

Automated detection of urgency and pressure tactics, credential/OTP requests, and attachment-bait phrases in subject and body.

Deep link analysis

Unmasks shortened URLs and identifies lookalike domains (e.g. paypa1.com). Flags IP-based links and domain mismatch between visible text and real destination.

SOC-ready reporting

One-click “Copy report” with risk level, score, indicators, and suspicious URLs so security teams can paste into tickets or SIEM.

Privacy first

All analysis happens locally in your browser. No data is sent to external servers.

How the risk score works

The 0–100 score is built from text and link rules. Higher score = higher risk. Levels: Low (0–29), Medium (30–59), High (60–100).

  • +10 Urgency/pressure
  • +20 Credential/OTP/login request
  • +10 Attachment bait phrases
  • +25 Domain mismatch
  • +25 IP-based URL host
  • +15 URL shortener
  • +15 Lookalike
  • +30 Dangerous scheme
  • +10 Non-HTTPS (http) link
  • +5 Many external link domains
